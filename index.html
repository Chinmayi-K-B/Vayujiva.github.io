About the Course

What is the course about?

Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA) is not just a branch of computer science; it is the very foundation of the field. Its principles extend beyond computer science and have applications in various domains, including science, business, and technology. This course fosters analytical thinking, equipping students with problem-solving strategies for real-world challenges.

What kind of data structures and algorithms have you studied?

Exploring data structures is like embarking on a journey through an intricate family of concepts. Arrays and strings resemble neat, orderly blocks of memory, while linked lists reflect interconnected paths of web pages. Stacks and queues evoke scenarios like biscuit stacks or waiting lines, while trees and tries open up a world of hierarchical relationships and efficient searches.

How do you connect the course with real-time applications?

Algorithms permeate our lives, guiding us home through GPS or managing massive datasets in e-commerce. They take complex challenges and create elegant solutions, optimizing, protecting, and personalizing the technology we rely on every day.
