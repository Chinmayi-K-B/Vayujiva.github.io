Observing Algorithmic Patterns in Nature

Iteration in Nature

Example: The recurring cycle of seasons is a perfect example of iteration. Like a loop in an algorithm, the progression from one season to the next happens repeatedly and predictably, much as a loop executes a set of instructions multiple times for different inputs.

Recursion in Nature

Example: The growth pattern of cabbage leaves, where new leaves form on top of older ones in layers, closely resembles recursion. Just like a recursive function that builds on smaller subproblems, each new layer of leaves is formed based on the previous ones, continuing the process until the plant reaches maturity.

Backtracking in Nature

Example: Ants searching for food provide a clear analogy for backtracking. Ants will explore various paths in search of food and, if unsuccessful, will backtrack to their nest to try a different route. Smart Parking Systems

Problem: Traffic congestion due to inefficient parking space management and lack of real-time availability information.

Solution: Smart parking systems that utilize technologies such as tries and queues to optimize parking availability and direct vehicles to open spots in real-time.

Functionality: A trie can store and retrieve available parking slots based on location and type (e.g., compact, regular, or handicapped) efficiently.
