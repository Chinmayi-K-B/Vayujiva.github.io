SDG Goal 11: Smart City Solutions

Indicator Information

  • Goal (SDG_GOAL): Goal 11: Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable.
  • Target (SDG_TARGET): Target 11.2: By 2030, provide access to safe, affordable, accessible, and sustainable transport systems for all, improving road safety, notably by expanding public transport.
  • Indicator (SDG_INDICATOR): Indicator 11.2.1: Proportion of population that has convenient access to public transport, by sex, age, and persons with disabilities.

Definitions and Concepts

Efficient management of urban transportation systems, such as parking and EV charging stations, is essential to reduce congestion and improve accessibility. Smart city solutions leverage technology to optimize resources, such as parking availability and real-time charging station management, ensuring sustainable urban mobility.

Identified Business Cases

1. Smart Parking Systems

Problem: Traffic congestion due to inefficient parking space management and lack of real-time availability information.

Solution: Smart parking systems that utilize technologies such as tries and queues to optimize parking availability and direct vehicles to open spots in real-time.

Algorithmic Applications:

Parking Slot Availability using Trie

Functionality: A trie can store and retrieve available parking slots based on location and type (e.g., compact, regular, or handicapped) efficiently.

Example Usage:

  • Each node in the trie represents a level of detail (e.g., city > parking lot > slot number).
  • Quick lookup for the nearest available parking slot in a specific area.

Dynamic Queue for Real-time Slot Allocation

Functionality: A queue can dynamically manage the order of vehicles waiting for parking slots.

Example Usage:

  • Vehicles are queued as they approach the parking area.
  • As slots become available, the queue ensures that the nearest or most eligible vehicle is allocated a spot based on priority (e.g., arrival time or special needs).

2. EV Charging Station Management

Problem: The need to efficiently manage electric vehicle charging stations to support the growing adoption of EVs and ensure optimal usage of charging resources.

Solution: Smart management of EV charging stations that utilizes data structures like tries and queues for real-time allocation and efficient utilization of charging slots.

Algorithmic Applications:

EV Charging Slot Availability using Trie

Functionality: A trie can efficiently store and retrieve the availability of EV charging slots based on location, type of station (e.g., Level 1, Level 2, or DC Fast Charging), and other factors.

Example Usage:

  • Each node in the trie represents a level of detail (e.g., city > charging station > slot number).
  • Quick lookup for the nearest available charging station or slot, considering factors like station type and availability.

Dynamic Queue for Real-time EV Slot Allocation

Functionality: A queue manages the order of vehicles waiting for an EV charging station slot, ensuring fair and timely access to charging resources.

Example Usage:

  • Vehicles are queued as they arrive at a charging station.
  • As slots become available, the vehicle at the front of the queue is allocated a charging slot.
